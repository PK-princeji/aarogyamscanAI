Critical Volumetric Pathologies

Our 3D engine is specifically trained to detect life-threatening anomalies where human visual fatigue often leads to critical diagnostic omissions.

Intracranial Hemorrhages (ICH)

Pathological Vector: Ruptured cerebral aneurysms or severe cranial trauma leading to epidural, subdural, or subarachnoid bleeding.

AI Intervention: In stroke protocols, "Time is Brain." Our neural network detects hyperdense blood pooling within milliseconds, flagging critical neuro-emergencies before the patient even leaves the scanner.

Pulmonary Embolism (PE)

Pathological Vector: Arterial blockages within the pulmonary vasculature, typically originating from Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT).

AI Intervention: Tracing the complex, branching structure of the pulmonary arteries across hundreds of CT Angiography slices is grueling. Our 3D-CNN highlights filling defects automatically, preventing fatal cardiac arrests.

Oncological Mass Staging

Pathological Vector: Malignant cellular proliferation forming complex, irregular tumors across hepatic, renal, or pulmonary systems.

AI Intervention: Utilizing advanced U-Net segmentation, AarogyamScanAI calculates the exact 3D volume, diameter, and density (Hounsfield Units) of the tumor, providing oncologists with objective data for surgical planning.

The Volumetric Data Crisis

Why modern radiology desperately requires AI assistance for CT Scans.

A. The Data Avalanche

A modern 256-slice CT scanner generates massive datasets (often 1000+ images per patient). Radiologists are forced to scroll through endless slices, leading to severe visual and cognitive overload.

B. The "Satisfaction of Search" Error

When a radiologist finds one major abnormality (e.g., a massive tumor), the brain often unconsciously stops looking for secondary issues (e.g., a tiny micro-fracture). AI suffers from no such psychological biases; it scans every voxel uniformly.

3D Convolutional Architecture

From raw DICOM ingestion to Volumetric Heatmaps.

01

DICOM Series Ingestion

The system parses the multi-frame DICOM file, extracting metadata and applying strict Hounsfield Unit (HU) windowing (e.g., Brain Window, Bone Window) to isolate specific tissue densities.

02

Spatial 3D-CNN Processing

Unlike 2D networks, our 3D-CNN filters move across the X, Y, and Z axes. This allows the AI to understand the continuous spherical shape of a tumor, rather than viewing it as disconnected flat circles.

03

Voxel Segmentation

The final layer outputs a mathematically precise 3D bounding box and a color-coded heatmap overlay, guiding the surgeon's scalpel with sub-millimeter accuracy.
