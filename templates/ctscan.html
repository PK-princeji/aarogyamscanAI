Primary Pathologies & Clinical Focus

Our 3D deep learning models target critical emergencies where time-to-triage is a matter of life and death. Detecting sub-millimeter anomalies across hundreds of CT slices is our specialty.

Intracranial Hemorrhage (ICH)

Clinical Manifestation: Ruptured blood vessels within the cranial vault, leading to hematoma expansion and fatal brain herniation.

AI Intervention: The network identifies hyperdense fluid collections instantly. By calculating the exact Hounsfield Units, it differentiates between acute blood and calcification, fast-tracking stroke patients for immediate surgery.

Pulmonary Embolism (PE)

Clinical Manifestation: A sudden blockage in the pulmonary arteries, usually caused by blood clots traveling from the deep veins (DVT).

AI Intervention: Scanning a CT Pulmonary Angiogram (CTPA) involves checking hundreds of vessels. Our AI traces the entire arterial tree in seconds, highlighting filling defects and calculating the right ventricular strain ratio.

Complex Orthopedic Trauma

Clinical Pathogenesis: Multi-fragmentary (comminuted) fractures of the spine, pelvis, or maxillofacial bones resulting from high-impact trauma.

AI Intervention: Traditional X-rays fail to show the true depth of shattered bones. Our AI performs automated 3D mesh reconstruction, mapping every microscopic bone fragment to guide orthopedic surgeons before they enter the operating room.

The Slice Overload Crisis

Why human cognition struggles with modern multi-detector CT scanners.

A. Data Avalanche

A standard modern CT scan produces anywhere from 300 to 1,000+ individual image slices. A human radiologist must scroll through these slices continuously, creating severe visual tracking fatigue and "inattentional blindness."

B. The 2-Millimeter Miss

Early-stage lung cancer nodules are often just 2 to 3 millimeters in size. When buried among complex lung blood vessels, the human eye frequently skips over them, delaying life-saving oncology treatment by months.

C. Emergency Triage Bottlenecks

In a trauma center, multiple critical CT scans arrive simultaneously. Without AI to instantly flag the "Brain Bleed" over the "Sprained Ankle", critical patients wait in the queue while their condition deteriorates.

3D Neural Architecture

How our volumetric engine processes raw tomographic data.

01

Hounsfield Unit (HU) Calibration

Raw DICOM matrices are ingested. The AI applies precise Window Leveling—setting bone to +1000 HU, water to 0 HU, and air to -1000 HU to isolate the target organ.

02

3D Convolutional Feature Extraction

Unlike 2D X-rays, CT scans require 3D CNNs (e.g., 3D U-Net). The network extracts features not just in length and width, but across the Z-axis (depth) to understand the true spherical volume of a tumor.

03

Multi-Planar Reconstruction (MPR)

The AI simultaneously evaluates the scan in Axial, Coronal, and Sagittal planes, ensuring that an anomaly hidden in one view is caught by the cross-referencing logic of another.

Ecological Sustainability & Medical Efficiency

CT Scanners consume massive energy. AarogyamScanAI optimizes the workflow to reduce waste.

The Traditional Bottleneck

  • Unnecessary Repeat Scans: Because of unclear manual reads, patients are often subjected to repeat CT scans, doubling their ionizing radiation exposure.
  • Contrast Dye Waste: Iodine-based contrast dyes are expensive and tough on the kidneys. Poor timing often leads to wasted dye.
  • Server Energy Drain: Local hospitals run massive, energy-hungry servers 24/7 just to render 3D images for doctors.

The AarogyamScanAI Resolution

  • Lower Dose, Higher Clarity: AI denoising algorithms allow CT scanners to run at ultra-low radiation doses, making scans drastically safer for pediatric patients.
  • First-Time Accuracy: Near-perfect initial detection eliminates the need for repeat scans, saving resources and patient health.
  • Cloud-Edge Efficiency: Processing is offloaded to highly optimized, sustainable cloud neural processors, cutting local hospital energy usage by 60%.
