Multi-Parametric Sequence Integration

Human eyes analyze one sequence at a time. AarogyamScanAI cross-references multiple MRI physics principles simultaneously to eliminate false positives.

T1-Weighted (T1W)

Physics Focus: Longitudinal relaxation time. Excellent for defining anatomical structures.

AI Utilization: Used as the baseline coordinate map for our spatial registration algorithms, allowing the CNN to map exact brain lobular boundaries.

T2-Weighted (T2W)

Physics Focus: Transverse relaxation time. Fluids appear hyperintense (bright).

AI Utilization: The primary vector for detecting edema (swelling) and inflammation around tumors or severe spinal disc herniations.

FLAIR Sequence

Physics Focus: Fluid-Attenuated Inversion Recovery. Nullifies cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) signals.

AI Utilization: Critical for identifying demyelinating plaques in Multiple Sclerosis, preventing the AI from confusing lesions with normal brain fluid.

DWI & ADC Maps

Physics Focus: Diffusion-Weighted Imaging tracks water molecule restriction at the cellular level.

AI Utilization: Enables the neural network to detect acute ischemic strokes within minutes of onset, far before structural changes are visible on standard T1/T2.

Advanced Neurological & Oncological Targeting

Our deep learning architecture targets the most complex, difficult-to-diagnose conditions that require pixel-by-pixel tracking over time.

Alzheimer's & Dementia

Pathological Vector: Progressive neurodegeneration leading to severe cortical atrophy and ventricular enlargement.

AI Intervention: AarogyamScanAI employs Hippocampal Volumetric Tracking. By calculating the exact cubic millimeter volume of the hippocampus across sequential MRI scans, the AI detects micro-atrophy years before clinical memory loss manifests.

Multiple Sclerosis (MS)

Pathological Vector: Autoimmune destruction of the protective myelin sheath covering nerve fibers, causing scarred plaques.

AI Intervention: Tracking the growth or shrinkage of MS lesions across time (spatio-temporal analysis) is a mathematical nightmare for humans. Our 3D U-Net isolates and counts every plaque on FLAIR sequences, generating an absolute disease progression metric.

Glioblastoma Multiforme (GBM)

Pathological Vector: The most aggressive malignant primary brain tumor, characterized by a necrotic core and extensive surrounding edema.

AI Intervention: Using multi-channel input (T1+Contrast, T2, FLAIR), the AI segments the tumor into three distinct zones: the enhancing core, the necrotic center, and the surrounding peritumoral edema. This dictates the exact radiotherapy targeting zone.

Spinal Cord Compression

Pathological Vector: Herniated intervertebral discs or spinal stenosis causing mechanical compression of the spinal cord or nerve roots.

AI Intervention: The network maps the entire cervical, thoracic, and lumbar spine, calculating the exact millimeter diameter of the spinal canal at every intervertebral level, instantly flagging critical stenosis points.

The MRI Data Avalanche

Why human cognition is fundamentally incompatible with modern multi-parametric MRI throughput.

A. Multi-Dimensional Overload

A single MRI exam consists of 4 to 6 different sequences, each containing hundreds of slices. A radiologist must mentally cross-reference a suspected lesion on T1, verify it on T2, and check for restriction on DWI. This mental juggling causes severe cognitive fatigue.

B. Lack of Quantitative Metrics

Human diagnosis is qualitative ("The tumor looks slightly bigger"). Oncology requires quantitative data. Manually drawing boundaries around a complex 3D tumor on 50 different slices to calculate its volume is practically impossible in a busy clinical setting.

C. The Reporting Backlog

Because MRI interpretation is incredibly complex, reports often take 3 to 7 days to reach the patient. In cases of acute spinal cord compression or aggressive tumors, this delay causes irreversible neurological deficits.

Hybrid 3D U-Net & Vision Transformer (ViT) Architecture

The bleeding edge of medical computer vision.

01

Multi-Channel Tensor Stacking

T1, T2, FLAIR, and DWI DICOM series are co-registered (spatially aligned) into a single 4D tensor matrix. Skull-stripping algorithms remove non-brain tissue to optimize computational load.

02

Local Feature Extraction (3D U-Net)

The convolutional contracting path of the U-Net captures high-resolution local textures (edges, micro-bleeds), while the expansive path accurately reconstructs the spatial boundaries of the anomaly.

03

Global Context (Vision Transformers)

Unlike CNNs which only look at local pixels, integrated Transformer attention mechanisms analyze the entire brain structure simultaneously, understanding long-range anatomical relationships (e.g., midline shift caused by a distant tumor).

04

Voxel-Wise Classification

The final output is a 3D segmented mask. Every single voxel is classified (e.g., 0=Healthy, 1=Edema, 2=Necrosis), generating an automated, mathematically absolute radiomics report.
